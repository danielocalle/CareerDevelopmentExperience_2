# My Reflection

## Activity Description

The expectation for this CYA assignment was to choose a career-development activity and provide proof of meaningful effort. I chose to document the stretch effort put into my current TA position in CPTS 321. My stretch-goal for this effort is to have a faster turnaround time on grading with an equally good level of student support, if not an even higher level of student support.

I took note of six homework assignments where I completed grading and submitted grades in under 24 hours, and one MT1 exam (that students took on a Friday) that was returned by the following Monday (`02/23/2026`). I also handled a significant number of follow-up or point-recovery conversations with students after initial grading. These activities increase my relative workload but provided students with a better opportunity to succeed in the course. 

## Technical Or Professional Decisions

To achieve this stretch-goal, I decided to focus mostly on increasing grade turnaround time, while also doing quick rubric re-checks and double checking with the TA/professor when I was uncertain or wasn't sure I was being as consistent as my goal. 

I also decided to be more flexible with allowing students to ask for point-recovery requests on the initial grading rather than considering the initial grading as final, even though this would work against me in the increased workload department. However, I figured this would improve many students' submissions and in the end allow them to be more successful in the course. As an example, I re-evaluated some of my own initial deductions and coordinated with the professor to adjust the points based on how I felt the rubric interpretation for that particular course emphasis wasn't quite aligned. 

## Contributions

This stretch effort was primarily my individual contribution. I personally handled grading responsiveness, follow-up communication, and regrade decisions for my assigned workload. I also collaborated with a TA team (four total TAs) and the professor to align rubric interpretation and maintain consistency across graders.

## Quality Assessment

I believe my performance was strong and I attained the two key goals of a faster turnaround time and improved student assistance, based on the measurable results and artifacts collected in the supporting write-ups.

If I repeated this experience, I would try and improve on logging and time tracking. The current write-ups do a reasonable job of portraying the work done but could be improved by actually logging the time (instead of estimating as used in the write-ups). Also, working more closely with the rest of the team of TA's could help us speed up our collective grading efforts. 

## Career Alignment

This experience helped both my short-term and long-term goals. It directly strengthened my communication and mentoring in a technical setting, as well as developed some important habits that would easily transfer into software engineering. This includes habits such as prioritization under time constraints, clear stakeholder communication, quality control and collaborative decision-making.
